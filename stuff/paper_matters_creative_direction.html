00 — VISION
The Creative Thesis
Paper is not a substrate. Paper is a civilization. Every sheet is a compressed record of forests, rivers, labor, intention, and time. This site will make people feel that.
The experience operates on three registers simultaneously: scientific (the material reality of cellulose, lignin, water), historical (printing press to pixelated screen), and sensory (the sound, weight, smell, texture that digital has always failed to capture). We are not designing a website. We are designing a memory — the feeling of paper in a world that forgot it.
The structural conceit: the entire experience is itself constructed from paper. The browser window is a sheet. The scroll is a slow unfolding. Sections tear apart. Typography bleeds. Everything physical is metaphor, and every metaphor is physically accurate.
HERO — The Convergence
The Press Room at Rest
"The moment before a sheet is born — suspended between potential and form."
Initial Load State (0–0s)
The screen is entirely black. A single sound: pressurized water — the wet slap of pulp hitting a wire mesh. Then nothing. The browser title bar reads: paper matters. Lowercase. Deliberate.
First 3 Seconds — Fibre Assembly
From the center of the screen, approximately 40,000 individual cellulose fibres — each rendered as a 1–4px white line segment with organic randomness in width and opacity — begin materializing outward. This is a WebGL particle system (Three.js, custom shader) running at 60fps. Each fibre has a unique rotation, length (4–22px), and opacity (0.3–0.9). They cluster, loosely aligning in the horizontal direction paper fibres actually align during manufacturing.
Seconds 3–5 — Sheet Formation
The fibres gradually compress into a flat plane — the paper sheet itself. As they settle, a warm cream color fills in between them using a displacement shader that bleeds outward from the center. The texture that forms is indistinguishable from 90gsm uncoated stock. The fibres don't disappear — they become the texture. The paper is made of its own fibres. This is scientifically accurate and visually impossible to look away from.
The Headline — Ink Bleed
Once the sheet is formed, the headline "PAPER MATTERS" prints itself using an ink-diffusion simulation. Individual letters don't appear letter-by-letter — ink bleeds into the paper from a central origin point, character by character filling in from the inside out. The leading P bleeds first, ink spreading through imaginary fibre channels in the paper, then the rest follow with 80ms staggering between characters. The font is Playfair Display at 140px. The bleed creates slight feathering at character edges — physically accurate to offset printing on uncoated stock.
Technology
Three.js WebGL, custom GLSL fragment shader for fibre simulation and ink diffusion
Duration
0–5s: particle assembly. 5–7s: ink formation. 7–9s: sub-elements fade in
Desktop Layout
Full viewport canvas. Paper sheet fills 80vw centered. Fibres extend to all edges, darker at periphery
Mobile Adaptation
Fibre count reduced to 8,000. Sheet fills full viewport. Headline at 52px. Vertical layout
Scroll Transition from Hero
As the user scrolls, the paper sheet itself begins to crumple — not fold, crumple — in the upper corners using a real-time cloth simulation (Three.js + MeshLine). The headline stays fixed briefly as the sheet warps beneath it, then the text physically de-forms with the paper before dissolving. The next section's content rises from below, itself printed on the reverse side of the sheet. The scroll is not navigating away from the hero — it's turning the sheet over.
01 — THE MANIFESTO
What Paper Remembers
A full-viewport cinematic text sequence. No images. Just words, space, and time.
Story Purpose
Establish the emotional register. Make the user feel something before they understand anything.
Visual Design
Warm cream background (the sheet). Lines of Playfair Display italic, 28–48px, left-aligned at an 8° tilt.
Animation
Each line prints in using GSAP SplitText on individual characters. Stagger 0.025s. Duration 1.2s per line. Ease: "power2.out"
ScrollTrigger
Pin the section for 4× viewport scroll distance. Each line triggers at 25% intervals. Lines fade on exit at -40deg X rotation.
The copy reads like a prose poem. Example lines: "A newspaper holds the temperature of 1963." / "The deed to your grandfather's house. The letter he wrote but never sent." / "Paper has always known things screens will never hold." Each line has a different tonal weight. Some are whispered at 18px. Some are declared at 56px.
Microinteraction
Hovering over any line causes it to briefly crumple — a CSS transform perspective skew that suggests the page curling — then springs back. Sound: a faint paper rustle (Web Audio API, < 100ms, volume 0.15). Optional, toggleable.
02 — THE MICROSCOPE
The Fibre World
A fully interactive microscope UI. The user zooms into a sheet of paper and discovers an entire universe.
The section opens with a bird's-eye view of a piece of paper. A circular lens element — like a loupe — appears with the cursor. As the user moves it over the paper, the area beneath magnifies to reveal the actual cellulose fibres (stylized as the WebGL simulation from the hero). At 3× zoom, individual fibres are visible. At 10× zoom, information cards appear: "This fibre is 2mm long. It began as a spruce tree in Sweden."
Technology
Canvas 2D + Three.js render target. Loupe uses CSS clip-path circle() + custom cursor
Interaction
Mouse position drives loupe. Scroll wheel controls magnification 1×–20×. Double click locks to position.
Mobile Adaptation
Pinch gesture replaces scroll zoom. Single tap locks. Static info cards replace hover states.
Memorable Quality
The paper that was abstract in the hero becomes real. Users discover it has depth, history, and specificity.
03 — THE ARCHIVE
A Century of Paper
A horizontal timeline that moves like a drawer being pulled from a cabinet.
The entire section is a horizontal scroll container triggered by vertical scroll (Lenis + GSAP horizontal ScrollTrigger). Twelve archival "documents" slide through — each is a full-height panel designed to look like a specific paper object from that era. 1440: illuminated manuscript texture. 1455: Gutenberg typeface. 1900: newspaper broadsheet. 1969: NASA mission report. Each panel has a different paper grain, print technique simulation, and typographic language.
ScrollTrigger
Vertical scroll drives horizontal X translation. 100vh vertical = 100vw horizontal movement. Pinned container.
Panel Transitions
Between panels, a brief flash of white — the gap between sheets — using GSAP fromTo opacity 0→1→0 in 120ms
Depth System
Each panel has 3 depth layers (background texture, content, foreground grain) moving at different scroll speeds: 0.85×, 1×, 1.15×
Typography
Each era uses historically accurate type — woodcut for 1455, hot metal for 1900, photocomp for 1970, digital for 1990
04 — THE PROOF
The Myth Dissolution Engine
Common myths about paper, printing, and pulp — destroyed in real time, on screen, in print.
A typographic statement appears — "Paper production destroys forests." — printed large in bold. The user interacts (hover or scroll) and the statement physically redacts itself: a red editorial correction line strikes through the text, and corrected text prints in underneath, sourced and footnoted. The visual language is a manuscript under editorial review.
GSAP Animation
Strike-through: GSAP DrawSVG on a horizontal line, duration 0.4s, ease "power3.inOut". Correction text: SplitText stagger-in.
Visual Design
Red correction marks reference copy-editing marks. Small superscript footnote numbers. Marginalia in ink-smear handwriting font (Caveat).
Microinteraction
Footnote hover opens a small paper slip (CSS translateY + scale + box-shadow) with citation. Clicks fold and unfold like origami.
Emotional Goal
Shift the user's relationship to paper from passive to intellectually engaged. Trust through transparency.
05 — THE ANATOMY
What a Sheet Is Made Of
An exploded diagram of a sheet of paper — not a chart, not an infographic. A physical disassembly in motion.
A sheet of paper fills the screen in cross-section, rendered in extreme detail. Then it begins separating — the layers slowly drifting apart like a time-lapse of a book being pressed. Each layer is labeled: surface sizing, base coat, cellulose fibre matrix, mineral filler, bottom sizing. Labels fly in from the margins using GSAP MotionPath along curved leader lines. The distance between layers increases as the user scrolls, making the anatomy increasingly readable.
GSAP Architecture
ScrollTrigger driving layer Y translation. Layer 1: 0→-120px. Layer 2: 0→-60px. Layer 3: stationary. Layer 4: 0→60px. Layer 5: 0→120px.
Label Animation
GSAP MotionPath. Each label follows a curved SVG path from offscreen-right to its anchor. Duration 0.6s, staggered by 0.15s.
Hover State
Hovering a layer highlights it with a warm amber overlay and plays a unique texture animation — fibres moving, mineral particles shifting.
Mobile Adaptation
Layers stack vertically. Tap to expand each layer's detail card. Horizontal swipe moves between layers.
SIGNATURE MOMENTS
Five Unforgettable Instants
WOW MOMENT 01
The Folding Transition Between Sections 1 → 2
As section 1 exits, the entire viewport page physically folds from the bottom-right corner — a real-time Three.js cloth simulation. The reverse side reveals a completely different paper texture and color. The next section's content was always on the back. The user has just turned a page. The fold takes 1.4s and is triggered by scroll velocity. Fast scrolling = aggressive fold. Slow scrolling = deliberate, graceful. The sound: a clean, dry page turn at 0.2 volume.
WOW MOMENT 02
The Fibre Scanner — Live Haptic Analysis
In Section 02, when the user double-clicks to lock the microscope view, a scanning animation runs — a luminous horizontal line sweeps across the field of view, and as it passes, individual fibres glow one by one, and a sidebar panel begins printing fiber statistics in real time: length, species of tree origin, geographic region, mill name. The data is fictional but formatted as if from a real electron microscope report. The effect of having information generated about something as humble as paper fibres is profound.
WOW MOMENT 03
The Archive Panel Transition — The Paper Gap
Between each archive panel in Section 03, the horizontal scroll passes through approximately 40px of pure white — not a fade, a physical gap between sheets. During this gap, no content exists. The screen is just white. It lasts 80ms. But it functions as a breath — a reset — between entirely different historical worlds. The user becomes Pavlovian: they begin to anticipate these gaps. They learn the rhythm of paper.
WOW MOMENT 04
The Origami Component — Watch Ideas Fold Into Form
Section 08 (The Product Story) opens with a flat die-cut paper blank — the precise unfolded template for a paper box. As the user scrolls, this flat form slowly assembles itself using GSAP Flip — each face of the box rising along its crease line in sequence, creating the finished 3D paper box in real time. The box contains the product. The unboxing is the story. Three.js with OrbitControls lets the user rotate it afterward. This is 11 seconds of animation time for 8 scroll-vh input.
WOW MOMENT 05
The Final Section — The Blank Sheet
The very last section is a full-viewport, perfectly white sheet of paper. Nothing else. Then the cursor becomes a pen. As the user moves, they draw on the paper — a thin, slightly imperfect inky line using Canvas 2D with pressure simulation from mouse velocity. The first thing every user does is write their name. Below the sheet, faint text appears: "Every piece of paper begins like this." The browser tab title changes to their cursor's last character. This is memorable forever.
06 → 15 — FULL SECTION BREAKDOWN
The Complete Journey
06 — The Forest Origin
A living forest rendered in Three.js — stylized, not photorealistic. Trees as geometric forms with paper-texture bark. Falling leaves behave as paper scraps (rigid body physics, Cannon.js). Scroll reveals the story of sustainable forestry. Ambient sound: wind, birdsong, distant chainsaw that cuts out intentionally. Emotional goal: restore the natural dignity paper deserves.
07 — The Mill Process
A side-scrolling industrial diagram — the paper mill as an isometric mechanical illustration. Not a flow chart. An actual machine you can see operating. Each stage pulses with activity using synchronized GSAP timelines (conveyors moving, rollers spinning, steam rising). Clicking any machine component opens a paper slip with process detail. The typography is engineering-spec notation. Emotional goal: awe at the complexity behind something "simple."
08 — The Origami Product Story
The Wow Moment 04 sequence. A flat die-cut template assembles into a product container. The scroll-driven origami sequence. OrbitControls exploration afterward. CTA appears printed on the bottom panel of the box as if it was always there.
09 — The Grain Direction
An interactive physics demonstration: paper folded with and against the grain. WebGL cloth simulation. The user drags a corner. Against-grain: irregular buckling, distressed. With-grain: clean, controlled. This single interactive teaches more about paper than any text could. Visceral, physical, immediate.
10 — The Weight System
A visual scale — like an apothecary's balance — showing different paper weights from 60gsm to 350gsm. Piling paper onto the scale using drag-and-drop. Each weight changes the visual of the sheet (opacity, shadow depth, flex when held). Numbers float as typeset weight designations (60gsm, 80gsm, 120gsm) using a beautiful monospaced weight. Emotional goal: make the user reach for paper.
11 — The Printing Ink
Four CMYK ink droplets fall onto a wet sheet of paper in slow motion. Canvas 2D fluid simulation. They spread, bleed, intermingle at edges. Where they overlap, secondary colors bloom. The science of ink and paper absorption — halftone dots forming, ink trapping, paper holdout — visualized with total physical accuracy. A slider controls paper coatedness: uncoated → silk → gloss. The fluid behavior changes accordingly.
12 — The Sound of Paper
A full-viewport paper instrument. The screen is a sheet. The user touches/clicks different zones — each produces a different paper sound: sharp crinkle, deep thud of thick stock, dry whisper of tissue, the crack of a fold. A visual ripple radiates from each touch point like a plucked string. Web Audio API synthesizer constructed from recorded paper samples. No UI chrome. Just paper and sound and sensation.
13 — The Environmental Story
Data visualization as hand-illustrated infographic, not chart-library defaults. Trees planted per ton of paper produced, rendered as growing SVG trees in real time. Water used in production visualized as droplets counting down. Carbon sequestration shown as ink darkening a sheet over time. All numbers accurate and cited. Emotional goal: counterintuitive pride in responsibly sourced paper.
14 — The Testimonials
Quotes from unlikely sources: architects, surgeons, musicians, writers — all describing the irreplaceable role of paper in their work. Each quote is designed as a typographic specimen: different paper stocks, different printing techniques, different letter forms. The quotes are themselves artifacts. Not testimonial cards with profile photos. Letters. Printed things. Emotional goal: paper as cultural foundation.
15 — The Blank Sheet (Final)
The Wow Moment 05 sequence. Drawing on the last sheet. The cursor-pen. The name. The moment. The CTA below the fold. The sound of a pen on paper, synthesized from recorded Foley. The ending is not a sales pitch. It is an invitation.
SIGNATURE COMPONENTS
Custom Invented Elements
The Caliper
A precision tool UI rendered as a physical micrometer caliper — the actual instrument used to measure paper thickness (caliper). Dragging its jaws controls the paper weight slider. At extreme values, the caliper audibly clicks at standard weight stops. The metaphor is perfect: the caliper measures what the user is exploring. Precision, craft, science.
The Watermark Revealer
Hold your cursor over any section background and the paper's hidden watermark reveals itself — visible only with backlighting. Achieved using SVG mask animation triggered by cursor proximity. The watermark shows the Paper Matters logo in negative space, formed by paper thickness variation. As if the logo was always there, always embedded in the material. Deeply satisfying on discovery.
The Deckle Edge Navigator
The section navigation is a vertical strip of hand-torn paper edges — deckle edges, the natural frayed border of handmade paper. Each torn section is a different paper stock (colored, textured). Clicking a section tears you forward with a cloth simulation. The nav is simultaneously the most beautiful and most technically complex nav ever built for a paper site.
The Compositor's Stick
In the typography-focused sections, hovering over text activates the compositor's stick — a thin horizontal guide that slides under lines of text like a typesetter's line gauge. It shows baseline, cap height, x-height, descender markers. An educational overlay that makes the user see typography as craft. The metaphor honors 600 years of typesetting history.
The Pulper
A destructive-creative CTA element. When the user reaches the final CTA, there is a secondary option: "Pulp it." Clicking this sends the current page layout into a pulping simulation — text fragments scatter, paper layers shred, and from the chaos new fibres emerge and re-form as a fresh sheet with just the CTA visible. "Everything paper can become paper again." The most dramatic possible expression of the brand's sustainability message.
TYPE SYSTEM
The Typographic Architecture
Playfair Display
Editorial headline — 700 weight, tight tracking
EB GARAMOND — BODY & NARRATIVE COPY
EB Garamond for running text. Modelled on the typefaces of Claude Garamond, 1530. Long-form reading. The paper equivalent of a serif pressed in lead.
Display Headlines
Playfair Display 700. 80–140px. -2px tracking. Line height 1.0–1.05. Used for section titles and hero.
Editorial Sub-heads
Playfair Display 400 Italic. 24–36px. Used for section introductions and pull quotes.
Body Copy
EB Garamond 400. 16–18px. 1.75 line height. 65-char max line length. The reading experience is the product.
Labels & Data
DM Mono 300. 9–11px. 3–4px letter spacing. All caps. Used for spec data, measurements, timestamps.
The rhythm is editorial — modular scale 1.618 (golden ratio). Sections breathe. Nothing is crowded. White space is paper space: it is not empty, it is the substrate. Every spacing decision is the equivalent of choosing the right weight of paper for the content it carries.

GSAP ARCHITECTURE
The Motion Design System
Core Easing Philosophy
Paper moves with physical memory. It resists, then releases. It doesn't bounce — it settles. The primary easing function across the site is a custom bezier curve approximating paper inertia: ease: "power3.inOut" for large movements, "power2.out" for entrances, "power4.in" for exits. No bounce easings except in the Pulper component where chaos is intentional.
ScrollTrigger Architecture
Global Lenis → GSAP Ticker
Lenis handles smooth scroll, feeding RAF to GSAP. Every ScrollTrigger is refreshed on resize. Scroll velocity is captured for physics-reactive elements (the hero page crumple speed, the fold animation aggression).
Pin System
Sections 01, 03, 05, 08 use pinning. All other sections use scrub:true for scroll-driven animations. Pin releases always use a crisp snap — no floating.
Stagger Library
A global stagger constant: 0.025s for character-level, 0.08s for word-level, 0.15s for element-level. Consistent across all SplitText animations. The timing is matched to average reading speed.
Three.js Integration
Three.js renders into a background canvas element. GSAP timeline controls Three.js uniform values (shader time, opacity, displacement amounts) via proxy objects. The two systems are architecturally separate but temporally synchronized.
GSAP FLIP for Layout Transitions
Section 08 origami sequence uses GSAP FLIP exclusively. The die-cut template's faces are tracked before and after CSS 3D transform application — FLIP handles the interpolation, ensuring physically correct fold paths without manual keyframe management.
Sound Design
Paper Rustle
Triggered on hover/SplitText entrance. 40–80ms. 0.10–0.18 volume. Pitch randomized ±15%.
Page Fold
Triggered on page-fold transition. 800ms clean, dry sound. Volume 0.25. No reverb.
Ink Press
On major SplitText reveals. Deep, satisfying thud + creak. 200ms. Volume 0.20.
Scanner Hum
Section 02 fibre scanner. Looping sine wave tone. Cuts out precisely on scan completion.
Pen on Paper
Section 15 drawing. Velocity-responsive — faster = scratchier, slower = smoother. Real Foley recording.
Global Toggle
Sound is opt-in. A small speaker icon (muted by default) unlocks audio globally. Never autoplay without consent.
MOBILE EXPERIENCE
Paper in Your Hand
Mobile is not a downgrade. It is a different material: smaller, more intimate, held in the hand like a book.
Hero Adaptation
Reduced fibre count (8,000). Full-screen portrait canvas. Gyroscope parallax on supporting devices — tilt the phone to see the paper's sheen shift.
Gesture System
Swipe left/right = page turn (section 03 archive). Pinch = zoom (section 02 microscope). Long press = material info tooltip. Tap = primary interaction.
Section 15 on Mobile
The blank sheet uses touch events. A finger on glass drawing on paper — the most intimate version of the moment. Haptic feedback (if supported) on first mark.
Performance
WebGL features degrade gracefully. Three.js scenes have mobile-specific particle counts, shadow maps disabled, reduced draw calls. 60fps target maintained via RAF throttling on older devices.
USER JOURNEY
From First Pixel to Final Mark
0–5s: Wonder
Fibres assemble. The user realizes this site is different. They lean forward. They slow down. They haven't scrolled yet.
5–30s: Discovery
The manifesto lines print. The user reads slowly. They hover and hear the rustle. They understand: this is not a website. It is a place.
30s–2min: Immersion
The archive, the microscope, the anatomy. The user is inside the world. They pause the scroll. They explore the Fibre Scanner. They zoom the microscope to 20×. They have forgotten they were shopping.
2–4min: Conversion
The origami box. The grain demonstration. The ink simulation. The user now understands what paper actually is. The brand's expertise is not claimed — it has been demonstrated.
4–6min: Transcendence
The blank sheet. They draw their name. The text appears: "Every piece of paper begins like this." They screenshot it. They share it. They remember this for years.
Exit Intent: The Pulper
For users who hesitate at the CTA: the Pulper. Destroy the page and rebuild it. The brand's message as destructive-creative act. Conversion is the second action after pulping. It always is.
